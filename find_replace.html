Find and replace

Designed the experience for performing bulk refactor in Postman



This story highlights the importance of 'keep trying until you succeed'.

Problem

Finding and replacing multiple instances of any text, code, or variable in Postman was time consuming

Users work with large amounts of data in Postman. However, there was no support to find and replace text, code, or variables in bulk. Users had to update things one by one, which was time consuming and not productive.

Find and replace was a long awaited feature in Postman - we had been getting several feature requests over the years around supporting bulk refactoring in the app.

The challenge

Support find and replace (bulk refactoring) in Postman

Our goal for the project was to support finding and replacing text, code, and variables in bulk in Postman. The higher level goal was to reduce the time that users spent in finding and replacing things one by one, and thus increasing their productivity.

Responsibilities

My role

I was the sole designer on this feature, and worked in close collaboration with one developer.

As the only designer working on this feature, my responsibilities included:

  • Understanding the underlying need of the users
  • Learning about bulk refactoring in code
  • Wireframing: Understanding the Postman interface and creating wireframes to identify the best real estate to introduce this feature
  • Creating high-fidelity mocks and prototypes for the final solution
  • Working closely with engineers to ensure high-quality implementation
  • Writing documentation

Solution

Introducing Find and Replace in Postman

Find anything across the Postman app

To find something, simply click on the “Find” icon in the status bar or hit ‘CMD+SHIFT+f’ on Mac or ‘CTRL + SHIFT + f’ on Windows and Linux devices. Enter your search query and hit 'Enter' or click on the Find button.

The process

Understanding the 'Why', 'Who', 'When' and 'Where'

To start off, I wanted to deeply understand the goal (Why), the users (Who), and the needs and context of the users (When and Where).

Core learning

Biggest learning from this project

My major learning from this project was realising and understanding the iterative nature of design. Sometimes it can take several iterations to reach a design which finally 'works'. Creating, sharing with team members, receiving feedback and iterating is the key. There's always scope for improvement.
